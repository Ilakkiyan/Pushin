# Daily briefing & the action bar

## Daily briefing

A slim banner sits above the calendar with an at-a-glance read on your day:

- how many **events** you have today,
- how many tasks are **due** (today or overdue),
- how much **focus time** is already blocked.

Below the summary it lists the due/overdue tasks so the most pressing work is front and center. The briefing is assembled deterministically from your calendar and tasks — no AI involved — and it simply doesn't appear on a clear day. Dismiss it with the **×**.

## The command bar (⌘K → Run)

The [command palette](./planning) (`Cmd/Ctrl-K`) isn't just for search and navigation — it's also an action bar. Type a natural-language instruction and choose **Run: "…"** to send it straight through the planner, from anywhere in the app:

- *"move the dentist to 3pm"*
- *"block 2 hours tomorrow morning for the report"*
- *"cancel Friday's standup"*

Pushin runs it and shows a one-line summary of what changed. It's the same on-device planner as the chat, one keystroke away.
